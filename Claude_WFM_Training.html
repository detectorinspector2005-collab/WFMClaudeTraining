What is Claude?

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. Unlike a search engine, Claude reads your full context, understands what you are trying to do, and produces structured output you can use immediately — emails, reports, schedules, analyses, and working tools.

For our WFM team, Claude is most useful as a drafting partner, a data interpreter, and a tool builder. It does not replace your judgment — it handles the time-consuming parts so you can focus on decisions.

📄

Reads & Understands

Upload Excel files, PDFs, or paste raw data. Claude reads context and gives you structured insights instantly.

✉️

Writes & Communicates

Emails, incident reports, proposals, coaching notes — it drafts everything in your professional voice.

🔧

Builds & Automates

Creates tools, dashboards, and reports you can actually use — no coding skills required.

How to access Claude

Go to claude.ai in any browser. Create a free account using your email. The free plan is enough to complete all exercises in this training and handle most daily WFM tasks.

Free vs Pro: The free account supports text conversations and file uploads. The Pro plan unlocks larger file sizes, longer conversations, and priority access — useful for heavy use like large Excel analysis or building complex tools.

One important thing before you start

Claude does not remember your previous conversations. Every new tab is a fresh start. This is why setting your preferences (covered in Module 4) is so important — it gives Claude a consistent starting point every time without you having to re-explain your role.

Setting your preferences

⚙️ Profile Setup
⏱ 5 min + exercise

Tell Claude who you are — once

Preferences are instructions you save in your Claude account. Once set, Claude applies them to every response automatically. You never have to re-explain your role, format preference, or focus areas again.

🧠

Who You Are

Your job title, company, team, and level of expertise. This gives Claude context before you type a single word.

💬

How You Want Answers

Bullet points or paragraphs? Concise or detailed? Formal or conversational? Claude will match your style.

🔍

Your Focus Areas

The topics Claude should assume are relevant: scheduling, AHT, SLA, adherence, contact center operations.

Where to set it

In claude.ai: click your profile icon in the top right → Settings → Profile → paste your preferences text → save.

Example preference prompt you can copy and adapt:

"I am a WFM team member at Detector Inspector Philippines working in a contact center. I manage scheduling, reporting, and operations. Always respond in bullet points unless I ask for something else. Be concise and professional. Focus on WFM topics: AHT, SLA, adherence, overbreak, aux utilization, scheduling, and contact center operations."

The 4-part prompt framework

✏️ Core Skill
⏱ 6 min + exercise

The quality of your output depends on the quality of your input

Claude produces better results when your prompt includes four things: context about who you are, a specific task, the format you want, and the tone that fits. You do not always need all four — but when a response misses the mark, the fix is almost always one of these.

01

Context

Who you are and what situation you are in.

"I am a WFM Supervisor at a contact center in the Philippines..."
02

Task

What you specifically need — be as precise as possible.

"Write an escalation email to IT about a network outage that affected 80 agents."
03

Format

How you want the answer presented.

"Give me a bullet list / table / one paragraph summary."
04

Tone

The style and voice that fits your use case.

"Professional but direct. Keep it under 150 words."

Claude refines as you go

If the first output is not right, you do not start over. Stay in the same thread and say what to change: "Make it more concise", "Add a table", "Change the tone to be more direct." Claude holds the full conversation context and adjusts.

Pro tip: A follow-up in the same thread is often more powerful than a brand new prompt. The second message builds on everything Claude already knows from the first.

What makes a prompt strong

Look at each strong prompt — they all share the same pattern: specific role as context, a concrete and scoped task, a stated output format, and either an implied or explicit tone. The weak prompts have none of these.

The test: Before sending a prompt, ask — could someone else read this and know exactly what output I need? If not, add more specifics.

Know what Claude can and cannot do

⚡ Capabilities
⏱ 4 min read

Set the right expectations

Understanding Claude's boundaries will save you time. Knowing what it handles well helps you delegate confidently. Knowing its limits prevents frustration.

Claude Can

  • Read and analyze uploaded files (Excel, PDF, CSV)
  • Write emails, reports, proposals, coaching notes
  • Build single-file HTML tools and dashboards
  • Explain data and suggest data-driven decisions
  • Refine and iterate based on your feedback
  • Understand WFM terms (AHT, SLA, Occupancy)

Limitations to Know

  • Does not retain memory across different conversations
  • Cannot access internal systems unless you paste the data
  • May make errors — always verify critical outputs
  • Knowledge has a cutoff date for recent events
  • Does not replace human judgment on sensitive decisions
  • Avoid sharing sensitive personal data or passwords

The memory point deserves extra attention

This is the most common source of confusion for new users. Every time you open a new conversation tab, Claude starts completely fresh. It has no idea what you discussed yesterday or even five minutes ago in a different tab.

What to do: Keep related tasks in the same thread. For recurring work, paste your preferences at the start of each new conversation, or set them in your profile so Claude always has context from the start.

How long do tasks actually take?

⏱ Time Estimates
⏱ 3 min read

Real numbers from our own builds

These estimates are from actual work done by this team using Claude. The first few times will take longer as you get comfortable with prompting style. After a week of regular use, Basic tasks feel almost instant.

TaskEst. TimeLevel
Draft a professional email (escalation, request, policy)30 secondsBasic
Write an incident report or executive summary1-2 minutesBasic
Analyze an uploaded Excel file and give insights2-3 minutesIntermediate
Build a formatted report from raw data or notes5-10 minutesIntermediate
Create a PowerPoint deck with content10-15 minutesAdvanced
Build a single-file HTML tool (scheduler, dashboard)30-60 minutesAdvanced
Build an Agent Metrics Dashboard (full, live data)2-4 hours (iterative)Expert

The learning curve is short

Most team members reach comfortable speed with Basic tasks within 2-3 days of regular use. Intermediate tasks become natural within a week. The key is using Claude for real tasks every day — not just practicing in isolation.

Recommendation: Start with one Basic task per day this week. Replace one email or report you would normally write from scratch. By Friday, it will feel natural.
